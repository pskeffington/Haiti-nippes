# Structured abstract

## Title

Improving maternal-health access in Haiti's Nippes Department: A source-tagged rural referral and emergency obstetric care framework

## Background

Haiti continues to face severe maternal-health challenges, including high maternal mortality, incomplete skilled birth attendance, fragile health financing, and uneven access to emergency obstetric care. These national constraints are especially important for rural departments such as Nippes, where road disruption, post-disaster recovery needs, WASH fragility, and facility-readiness gaps can delay timely care for obstetric emergencies.

## Objective

This project develops a source-tagged maternal-health access framework for Nippes Department, Haiti, to support commune-level prioritization of rural maternal referral constraints and emergency obstetric care readiness.

## Population and setting

The setting is Nippes Department, Haiti, with analysis organized around communes and referral corridors. The priority populations are pregnant women, postpartum women, newborns, adolescent pregnant women, displaced women and girls, survivors of gender-based violence, and women with obstetric complications.

## Methods

The project uses an applied public-health policy analysis design. A source registry records national and departmental source leads for maternal mortality, skilled birth attendance, sexual and reproductive health needs, administrative geography, road disruption, WASH conditions, food insecurity, displacement pressure, and health-facility readiness. A typed maternal-health object model defines service categories, referral constraints, risk groups, source leads, facility or commune capacity records, and referral profiles. A commune-level maternal-access index is specified as an exploratory prioritization tool using source-tagged inputs for skilled birth attendance gaps, referral travel time, road disruption, facility-readiness gaps, WASH gaps, food insecurity, displacement pressure, and maternal referral constraints.

## Expected outputs

Expected outputs include a source registry, a structured maternal-health framework, a commune-level maternal-access input table, an exploratory maternal-access prioritization score, and policy recommendations for strengthening skilled birth attendance, emergency obstetric referral, facility readiness, WASH resilience, and continuity of care for women and newborns.

## Policy relevance

The framework supports practical planning by separating national Haiti maternal-health indicators from Nippes-specific analytic inputs. It can help identify where rural referral barriers, facility-readiness gaps, and infrastructure constraints should be prioritized before implementing or evaluating maternal-health interventions.

## Limitations

The proposed index is a planning and prioritization tool, not an official maternal mortality estimate or causal model. Departmental and commune-level claims require verification against dated public sources before final reporting. Placeholder input rows should not be interpreted as observed data, and no field verification is implied unless independently documented.
